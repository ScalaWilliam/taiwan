Kaohsiung

What a change from Tainan: we could finally see and use sidewalks.

Second largest city in Taiwan, Kaohsiung is well designed and chilled out.

However as soon as I got out the car, I began sneezing. Next day, I had a sore throat and later on headaches and fever. Perhaps we should've not stayed this long in Kaohsiung due to the pollution, which turned out to be 100 PSI.

Kaohsiung was super hot. Like really hot. Don't even bother coming out 11-3pm without an umbrella, sunblock, shades, water and a face mask (ok, this is not strictly necessary).

Most fun experience in Kaohsiung was a ride in the Cijin Coast Park on Cijin Island. Take a ferry using EasyCard there, to be greeted with access to electric buggies (?) of NTD 200/hour. They wanted to keep a form of ID as deposit which I got back afterwards. Also they wanted payment in advance. Now you can go at 28km/h and in shade, far better than walking or cycling!
